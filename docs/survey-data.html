8.1 Obtaining coefficients via regression modelling

If your data comes from a complex survey (i.e. stratified sampling, two-stage, clusters, etc.) then you should make your regression model reflect the sampling strategy that was used to bias your sample away from the population (Gelman 2007).

8.2 Obtaining population estimates via regression modelling

You should begin by forming a model as explained in Chapter 8.1 And then use this model make estimates for each group. Then perform a weighted average (Gelman 2007).

References

Gelman, Andrew. 2007. “Struggles with Survey Weighting and Regression Modeling.” Statistical Science 22 (2): 153–64. doi:10.1214/088342306000000691.
